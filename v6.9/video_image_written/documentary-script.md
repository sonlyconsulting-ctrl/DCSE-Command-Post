# PAR-TEE: THE CLUB THAT CHANGED THE GAME
## A Feature Documentary Script

**FADE IN:**

**AERIAL SHOT - FORT WAYNE, INDIANA - DAWN**
*Morning mist rises over Foster Park Golf Course as the sun breaks through the trees.*

**MORGAN FREEMAN (V.O.)**
In the heart of America's Midwest, where steel meets soul, a group of men would forge a legacy that transcended the game of golf itself.

**ARCHIVAL FOOTAGE - 1950s FORT WAYNE**
*Black and white footage shows factories, workers leaving shifts, Korean War veterans returning home.*

**MORGAN FREEMAN (V.O.)**
The year was 1955. Eisenhower was president, the Korean War had ended, and America was changing. But in Fort Wayne, Indiana, fourteen men were about to start a revolution of their own.

**INTERVIEW - JAMES WALKER JR. (80s)**
*(Son of founding member)*
My father came back from Korea like many of them did. They needed something... something to hold onto. Golf gave them that purpose.

**ARCHIVAL PHOTOS**
*Fourteen men standing outside American Legion on Hanna Street, golf clubs in hand.*

**HISTORIAN DR. SARAH THOMPSON**
The American Legion on Hanna Street might seem like an unlikely birthplace for a golf club, but for these men, it was perfect. It represented everything they stood for - community, service, and brotherhood.

**MONTAGE**
- Factory workers leaving Harvester
- Golf balls being hit at Foster Park
- Men laughing, keeping score
- Early tournament photos

**MORGAN FREEMAN (V.O.)**
They called themselves the Par-Tee Golf Club. But this wasn't your typical country club story.

**INTERVIEW - CLARENCE JOHNSON (70s)**
*(Local golf historian)*
Back then, golf was a segregated sport. Most clubs wouldn't let African Americans play. But Par-Tee... Par-Tee was different.

**ARCHIVAL FOOTAGE - 1969**
*Memorial Coliseum tournament preparations.*

**MORGAN FREEMAN (V.O.)**
In 1969, they did something extraordinary. They hosted a tournament that would change everything.

**INTERVIEW - CALVIN PEETE JR.**
*(Son of golf legend Calvin Peete)*
When my father got the invitation to play in Fort Wayne, he knew it was special. Here was a club saying, 'Come play with us. Your skill is what matters.'

**MONTAGE - 1969 TOURNAMENT**
- Players arriving at Memorial Coliseum
- Tournament play
- Famous "19th hole" celebration
- Newspaper headlines

**DIANE MATTHEWS (70s)**
*(First female member)*
When Jimmy White sponsored me for membership, some people thought it was controversial. But that's not how Par-Tee saw it. They saw a golfer who loved the game.

**SLOW MOTION**
*Diane hitting a perfect drive down the fairway, circa 1980s.*

**MORGAN FREEMAN (V.O.)**
But Par-Tee's vision extended beyond the fairways and greens.

**INTERVIEW - MICHAEL THOMAS**
*(Par-Tee Scholarship recipient, 2002)*
I was just a kid from south Fort Wayne with dreams of college. The Par-Tee Scholarship didn't just give me money for school - it gave me a community that believed in me.

**MONTAGE - SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM**
- Scholarship award ceremonies
- Students graduating
- Community service events
- Modern Par-Tee tournaments

**DR. ROBERT WILSON**
*(Social historian)*
What makes the Par-Tee story remarkable is how organic it was. They didn't set out to make history. They just did what they thought was right, one decision at a time.

**AERIAL SHOT - MODERN FOSTER PARK**
*Diverse group of golfers playing where it all began.*

**MORGAN FREEMAN (V.O.)**
Today, nearly seventy years after those fourteen men first gathered at the American Legion, their legacy lives on. Not just in the tournaments played or the scholarships given, but in the lives changed and the barriers broken.

**MONTAGE - MODERN PAR-TEE**
- Current members of all ages and backgrounds
- Youth golf clinics
- Community events
- Scholarship presentations

**FINAL INTERVIEW - JAMES WALKER JR.**
Those fourteen men... they just wanted to play golf. But they ended up teaching Fort Wayne - teaching all of us - about what's possible when you open your heart and your fairways to everyone.

**AERIAL SHOT - SUNSET OVER FOSTER PARK**
*Camera pulls back to reveal the entire course.*

**MORGAN FREEMAN (V.O.)**
Some say golf is just a game. But for fourteen men in Fort Wayne, Indiana, it became something more. It became a way to change the world, one swing at a time.

**FADE TO BLACK**
