DCSE V6 Video Production Governance

Status: OPERATIONAL | Version: 1.0.0 | Authority: Subordinate Addendum to Master Profile v6
Entity: DCSE Core | Date: 2026-04-13 | Sonly Flip Active | Classification: Internal Governance

1. Purpose

This document is the V6 video-production addendum for governed creation of video content, motion graphics, audio assets, and multimedia deliverables across all DCSE entities.

It exists to control production quality, brand consistency, tool-chain discipline, asset management, secrets handling, distribution safety, and release readiness while remaining subordinate to Master Profile v6.

This addendum supplements V6. It does not take away from it. It also inherits and must comply with the Product Creation Governance addendum where video assets are embedded in or delivered alongside governed product builds.

2. Authority and Relationship to V6

Master Profile v6 remains the primary operational authority.

This addendum exists because video production has creative, technical, and distribution guardrails that should not over-expand the main master profile or the Product Creation Governance addendum.

Doctrine stack for video production tasks:

  1. Master Profile v6 (primary authority)
  2. v6.5 HTML Governance Doctrine (entity firewalls, model-duty registry, agentic controls)
  3. Product Creation Governance (when video is part of a product build)
  4. This Video Production Governance (current document)

Digital DNA operates as the identity, extraction, processing, calibration, and output-control layer for all video production governed by this addendum.

3. Scope

This addendum governs:

  • Scripted and unscripted video content
  • AI-generated video (Sora, Freepik, and equivalent tools)
  • AI-generated images used as video assets (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude)
  • Voice-over and narration (ElevenLabs and equivalent)
  • B-roll creation and curation
  • Motion graphics and text overlays
  • Music and audio design (royalty-free, AI-generated, or licensed)
  • Video editing and post-production (PowerDirector and equivalent)
  • Thumbnail and preview image creation
  • Captions, subtitles, and accessibility compliance
  • Asset library organization and governance
  • Distribution across YouTube, website, LinkedIn, Instagram, and other platforms
  • Secrets and API key management for production tools

4. Video Build Doctrine

Before production begins on any video asset, define:

VIDEO BUILD DECLARATION (Required Before Production) • Target Entity: [DCSE / SC / SS / PS / TI / DCS] • Content Series: [e.g., SC Authority Series / SS Transformation / PS Empowerment] • Video Type: [Promo (<60s) / Medium (1–5 min) / Long-form (>5 min) / Short-form Vertical] • Target Platform(s): [YouTube / Website / LinkedIn / Instagram / Multi-platform] • Audience: [Primary demographic and decision tier] • Public/Internal Status: [Public-Safe / Internal-Only] • Voice Archetype: [Entity preset per Master Profile v6 Section 3] • Assigned Model(s): [Per Section 9 of this document] • Agentic Authorization Level: [1 — Supervised / 2 — Spot-Checked / 3 — Autonomous OK] • Prohibited Expansion: [What this video must NOT include] • Digital DNA Compliance: [Entity overlay confirmed / Voice archetype locked]

No video production should proceed as an open-ended generation exercise. Every video is a governed product.

6. GYTO Firewall in Video Production

Fixed Doctrine: GET YOUR THINK ON!™ (GYTO) must not appear in any public-facing video content — titles, thumbnails, on-screen text, captions, narration scripts, or metadata (tags, descriptions, closed captions). GYTO is restricted to internal use only. Any model generating a public-facing video script, storyboard, or asset must suppress GYTO automatically. If GYTO appears in a source prompt for a public video asset, the model must redact it and flag the occurrence.

7. Cinematic Production Standards

All DCSE video production aspires to cinema-grade quality within accessible tooling. The following are reference standards, not absolute requirements for every output. Scale to the video type and budget tier.

7.1. Visual Reference Standards

  • Resolution: 4K DCI minimum for long-form. 1080p acceptable for social-first short-form. 8K as aspiration for flagship content.
  • Frame Rate: 24 fps for cinematic delivery. 30 fps for web/social. 60 fps only for smooth-motion sequences where justified.
  • Color: DCI-P3 gamut preferred. HDR when platform supports it. Moody warm gradients as the DCSE default grade.
  • Camera Reference: ARRI Alexa 65 / RED Komodo with anamorphic lens characteristics as the reference aesthetic for AI-generated footage and motion graphics.
  • Cinematography Inspiration: Roger Deakins, Greig Fraser, Emmanuel Lubezki. Controlled light, purposeful shadow, depth through layering.

7.2. Audio Reference Standards

  • Format: WAV or ProRes for master files. AAC/MP3 for distribution.
  • Voice-over: Professional-grade AI voice (ElevenLabs or equivalent) or live narration. Third-person narrative only per Master Profile v6. Male/female alternating tones permitted.
  • Music: Minimal orchestral score as default. Entity-specific overrides per Section 5. Royalty-free or properly licensed only.
  • Mixing: Voice at -6dB to -3dB. Music bed at -18dB to -12dB under narration. No music competing with voice.

7.3. Negative Prompt Standards

For all AI-generated video and image assets, enforce these exclusions:

  • No bad anatomy, extra limbs, missing fingers, distorted faces, warped proportions
  • No low resolution, pixelation, poor lighting, unintentional motion blur
  • No brand marks in trivial, sarcastic, or dismissive contexts
  • No deviation from entity-specific color palettes
  • No text-in-image generation unless reviewed for accuracy (AI text rendering remains unreliable)

8. Production Pipeline

Every governed video follows this pipeline. Steps may be compressed for short-form content but not skipped.

8.1. Pre-Production

  1. Video Build Declaration (Section 4) completed and entity confirmed.
  2. Script Development: Narrative arc defined. Third-person voice confirmed. Key messages identified. Call-to-action determined.
  3. Storyboard or Shot List: Visual sequence mapped. Asset sources identified (library, AI-generated, or recorded).
  4. Asset Sourcing Plan: Each asset tagged with source tool, generation method, and licensing status.
  5. Voice-over Script: Finalized, reviewed for entity compliance, timed against target duration.

8.2. Asset Creation

  1. Primary Footage: AI-generated video (Sora, Freepik) or recorded content. Shot per storyboard.
  2. B-Roll: Supplementary footage — lifestyle, business, technology, environment. AI-generated or sourced from governed asset library.
  3. Images: AI-generated (ChatGPT, Sora, Gemini, Claude) or sourced. Negative prompts enforced.
  4. Voice-over Recording: ElevenLabs or equivalent. Entity voice archetype confirmed before generation.
  5. Music Selection: Royalty-free or licensed. Entity-specific palette per Section 5.
  6. Motion Graphics & Text Overlays: Brand-consistent. Entity color tokens applied. No GYTO in public outputs.
  7. Thumbnails: Created per platform requirements. Consistent with entity visual system.

8.3. Post-Production

  1. Assembly Edit: Primary footage, B-roll, and voice-over assembled in timeline (PowerDirector or equivalent).
  2. Music & Audio Mix: Music bed placed. Voice levels balanced per Section 7.2.
  3. Color Grade: Entity-specific palette applied. Moody warm gradients as DCSE default.
  4. Text & Graphics: Lower thirds, overlays, captions inserted. Typography consistent with entity tokens.
  5. Captions & Subtitles: Generated and reviewed for accuracy. Required for all public-facing content (accessibility compliance).
  6. Review Cut: Full video reviewed against Video Build Declaration before export.

8.4. Export & Distribution

  1. Master Export: Highest quality for archive. 4K/ProRes or equivalent.
  2. Platform Exports: Format-specific renders per distribution targets (YouTube, website embed, LinkedIn, Instagram Reels, etc.).
  3. Metadata: Title, description, tags, thumbnail, captions packaged per platform requirements. GYTO suppressed from all public metadata.
  4. Scheduling: Publication timing aligned with content calendar and entity strategy.

11. Asset Library Governance

Video assets must be stored, tagged, and retrievable. An ungoverned asset library is a liability.

11.1. Storage Structure

  • Master Archive: Google Drive — DCSE_Operations/Video_Assets/
  • Folder Structure: Organized by entity, then by content series, then by asset type (footage, images, audio, graphics, exports).
  • Naming Convention: [Entity]-[Series]-[AssetType]-[Version]-[Date] — e.g., SC-Authority-BRoll-v1-20260413
  • Local Working Files: Local machine during active production. Moved to Drive archive upon project completion.

11.2. Asset Tagging

Each asset should carry metadata sufficient for retrieval and reuse:

ASSET TAG (Minimum Fields) • Entity: [SC / SS / PS / TI / DCSE] • Content Series: [e.g., SC Authority Series] • Asset Type: [Footage / B-Roll / Image / Audio / VO / Graphic / Music / Export] • Source Tool: [Sora / ChatGPT / ElevenLabs / PowerDirector / etc.] • License Status: [Royalty-Free / Licensed / AI-Generated-Commercial-OK / TBD] • Reuse Status: [Reusable / Project-Specific / One-Time] • Version: [v1.0 / v1.1 / etc.]

11.3. Retention and Deprecation

  • Master exports retained indefinitely.
  • Working project files retained for 90 days after final export, then archived or deleted.
  • Deprecated assets (superseded versions) retained in archive with deprecated tag, not deleted.
  • Assets with unclear licensing status must be flagged and not used in public-facing content until resolved.

12. Secrets and API Key Management

Video production relies on external services requiring API keys, subscription credentials, and account access. These are governed infrastructure, not convenient strings.

  • Never hardcode API keys (ElevenLabs, Sora, Freepik, Canva) into scripts, prompts, shared documents, or public repos.
  • Store credentials in a secure password manager or, for Wix-integrated workflows, in Wix Secrets Manager with backend-only access.
  • Document which tools require credentials and how they are consumed.
  • Separate subscription credentials from production credentials.
  • Review API key exposure risk during preflight (Section 14).

14. QA and Preflight

Every governed video must pass preflight checks before publication.

14.1. QA Checklist

  • Entity and Digital DNA compliance confirmed
  • Voice archetype matches target entity
  • GYTO absent from all public-facing surfaces (title, description, tags, on-screen text, captions, narration)
  • Captions/subtitles present and accurate
  • Audio levels balanced (voice over music, no clipping)
  • Color grade consistent with entity palette
  • No AI-generation artifacts (distorted faces, extra limbs, garbled text)
  • Thumbnail created and entity-consistent
  • Music licensing verified
  • No secret keys, credentials, or internal references visible in output
  • Duration matches target format
  • Platform-specific export requirements met

14.2. Preflight Declaration

VIDEO PREFLIGHT (Required Before Publish) • Video Title: [Final title] • Entity Confirmed: [SC / SS / PS / TI / DCSE] • GYTO Suppression Verified: [Yes] • Captions Present: [Yes / N/A] • Music License Confirmed: [Yes — source noted] • AI Artifact Check: [Passed / Flagged items resolved] • Secret Exposure Check: [Passed] • QA Reviewer: [Human / Model name] • Agentic Level Respected: [1 / 2 / 3] • Approved for Publication: [Yes / No — reason]

15. Release Rule

No video should be considered release-ready merely because it renders and plays.

Release readiness requires:

  • Preflight Declaration completed (Section 14.2)
  • Entity firewall verified
  • Digital DNA compliance confirmed
  • GYTO firewall passed
  • Licensing for all assets confirmed
  • Human review completed for all Level 1 content
  • Distribution metadata finalized
  • Known limits documented where applicable

All public-facing video content is Agentic Level 1 — Supervised. No public video release without human review and approval.

16. Packet Guidance

When a governed video production task is requested, the ideal minimum packet is:

  1. Master Profile v6
  2. Model-specific V6 set
  3. This Video Production Governance addendum

Add the Product Creation Governance addendum only if the video is part of a larger product build (e.g., embedded in an HTML module or Wix page).

17. Non-Negotiables

  • No GYTO in public-facing video content
  • No entity contamination across video productions
  • No unlicensed music or assets in published content
  • No AI-generated content published without artifact review
  • No hardcoded secrets or API keys in scripts, prompts, or shared files
  • No video published without preflight declaration
  • No public release at Agentic Level 3
  • No silent scope drift in content series
  • No PS legal content treated as entertainment
  • No weakening of V6 governance

Content series definitions (Section 13) and tool-chain assignments (Section 10) are subject to operational updates as tools evolve and new series are launched. Updates must follow the additive-first standard per Master Profile v6 Section 11.
