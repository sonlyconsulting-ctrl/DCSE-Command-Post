CATEGORY 2: IS THIS LEGITIMATE? (Objection - Credibility & Differentiation)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Q: Is this coaching, therapy, or actual consulting?
A: It's none of those exclusively. The Critical Thinker's Journey™ is a methodology for building better thinking habits and strategic clarity. You won't receive personal therapy or medical advice. You won't get a therapist. You *will* get frameworks that help you make intentional decisions, recognize bias in your own thinking, and navigate complexity with more consciousness. If you need clinical mental health support, we're not the right fit—and we'll say so. If you need to think more clearly about your life or business, we are.

Q: How is SC different from other consulting firms?
A: Most consulting firms sell expertise—they tell you what to do. SC teaches you *how to think*—so you can make better decisions even when circumstances change. We're rooted in critical thinking methodology, not industry templates. We work with people in transition (career changers, solo entrepreneurs, people rebuilding), not just corporations. And we're transparent about uncertainty—we don't pretend to have all the answers. We help you test whether *your* answers can survive scrutiny.

Q: What makes The Critical Thinker's Journey™ different?
A: CTJ combines three elements most programs separate: logic (how to think), evidence (how to test), and meaning (how to live intentionally). It's not a quick fix or a motivational hack. It's a structured journey through 30 days of daily frameworks designed to expand your thinking and build confidence in your own judgment. It's based on decades of real-world decision-making, not academic theory alone.

Q: Is this supported by research or just opinion?
A: CTJ is grounded in critical thinking frameworks (logic, evidence evaluation, cognitive bias recognition) that are well-established in cognitive science and epistemology. We're not inventing new psychology. We're applying proven thinking methodologies to the specific challenges people face in transition, business, and life decisions. You'll encounter references to established concepts: deductive/inductive reasoning, confirmation bias, correlation vs. causation, mental models, and more. It's rigorous, but accessible.

Q: Who uses SC/SS services?
A: Professionals in transition (recently laid off, career-changing), solo entrepreneurs building clarity around growth, people navigating complex decisions, creative professionals wanting strategic frameworks, and anyone tired of making decisions from a place of overwhelm. We work across industries and backgrounds—healthcare, tech, legal, creative, nonprofit, business. If you're thinking critically about your direction, you're the right person.
